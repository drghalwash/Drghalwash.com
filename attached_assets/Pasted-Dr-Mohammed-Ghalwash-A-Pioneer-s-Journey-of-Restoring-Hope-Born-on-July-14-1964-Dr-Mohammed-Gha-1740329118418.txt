Dr. Mohammed Ghalwash: A Pioneer’s Journey of Restoring Hope
Born on July 14, 1964, Dr. Mohammed Ghalwash began his surgical journey with a simple yet profound belief: “Every patient deserves to live with dignity, not just survival.” Over four decades, this principle has guided his hands to rewrite the futures of thousands.

The Saudi Arabia Chapter: Redefining Hair Restoration
In the early 1990s, when hair transplantation was still emerging globally, Dr. Ghalwash pioneered the first successful follicular unit transplant (FUT) in Saudi Arabia. At a time when many viewed baldness as irreversible, he offered a revolutionary solution:

Handcrafted Results: Using single-blade knives and magnifying loupes, he meticulously transplanted follicles one by one, ensuring natural hairlines that mirrored patients’ original patterns.

A Quiet Revolution: His Riyadh clinic became a beacon for those silently battling hair loss—diplomats, teachers, fathers—all regaining not just hair, but self-assurance. One patient later wrote: “He gave me back the face I recognized in old photos.”

Egypt’s Replantation Trailblazer
Returning to Alexandria in the late 1990s, Dr. Ghalwash turned his focus to limb and digit replantation—a field then in its infancy in Egypt. His work transformed despair into hope:

Microsurgical Precision: With tools finer than eyelashes, he reconnected blood vessels and nerves in severed fingers, hands, and limbs. A 2001 case—reattaching a factory worker’s crushed dominant hand—allowed the man to resume providing for his family.

24/7 Dedication: Colleagues recall him rushing to the clinic at dawn for a farmer’s amputated thumb, later stating: “His sowing season couldn’t wait for hospital bureaucracy.”

Building a Legacy from 55m²
Starting in a modest 55m² clinic in Alexandria’s Mourad district, Dr. Ghalwash’s reputation grew through relentless commitment:

Expanding Access: By 2005, his upgraded 110m² facility introduced Egypt to advanced techniques like toe-to-thumb transfers for amputees, blending function with aesthetics.

Teaching Through Action: Young surgeons trained under his motto: “See the person, not just the injury.” Many now lead Egypt’s replantation and hair restoration programs.

Why Patients Trust Him
Proven Impact: Over 2,300 successful replantations and 6,500+ hair transplants, with outcomes studied in regional medical journals.

Holistic Care: Pre-surgery, he sketches incisions to align with patients’ lifestyles (e.g., avoiding visible scars for a bride’s upcoming wedding). Post-op, he personally oversees dressing changes, believing “recovery begins with trust.”

Adapted Innovation: While adopting robotic hair tools later in his career, he insists: “Technology assists hands, never replaces them.”

A Living Legacy
Now mentoring his son Dr. Khaled—himself a Harvard-trained microsurgeon—Dr. Ghalwash’s philosophy endures:

Accessible Excellence: Still accepting complex cases others deem “too risky,” like a 2023 scalp replantation for a child after a dog attack.

Silent Humility: His clinic walls display no diplomas, only patient letters. One reads: “You didn’t just reattach my arm—you reattached me to my life.”

In His Words:
“Surgery isn’t about miracles. It’s about showing up—day after day, stitch after stitch—until the person in the mirror matches the one in their heart.”
